# Scene 13 — Ritual Ceremony

The first silver sliver of moon pierced the indigo veil of twilight, striking the highest spire of the Jade Chrysanthemum Palace. Below, in the vast courtyard paved with obsidian and pearl, nine massive *tsuzumi* drums began their slow, resonant heartbeat. *Boom.* Thick coils of sandalwood and star-pine incense, heavy and sweet, snaked upwards from braziers, weaving a shimmering, aromatic veil that blurred the edges of reality. Shadows deepened, pooling around the edges where the court of Yokai gathered – silent kitsune elders in brocade, watchful tengu perched on eaves, inscrutable kappa with water glistening on their shells.

Through the undulating smoke, she emerged. The Fox-Queen-elect, Kitsune no Hikari, walked the path of scattered night-blooming flowers. Her nine tails, each tipped with moonlit silver, fanned out like the rays of a pale sun, barely disturbing the sacred haze. She wore only ash-dyed silk, symbolizing humility before the ancient powers. The drummers intensified their rhythm, a primal pulse that vibrated in the bones of all present, syncing with the rising moon. Reaching the obsidian dais, bathed now in full lunar radiance, she knelt. An elder kitsune, his fur bleached white by centuries, stepped forward, holding a scroll of birch bark inscribed with shimmering, unreadable glyphs.

Hikari lifted her face, eyes reflecting the cold lunar fire. Her voice, when it came, was both a whisper and a gong-strike, echoing with the weight of forgotten epochs as she spoke the Binding Oath. The ancient tongue crackled in the air like static, each syllable causing the incense smoke to writhe into ghostly kanji before dissolving. Ripples of raw power shimmered across the courtyard; unseen spirits sighed. As the final vow echoed into silence, the moon seemed to blaze brighter. A circlet of foxfire, cool and eternal, coalesced upon Hikari's brow – the crown of the Nine-Tailed Sovereign was claimed.
