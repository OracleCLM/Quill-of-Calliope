# Scene 04 — Lore Exposition

## The Whispering Treaty

**(Scene: A moonlit grove where ancient stone pillars stand crookedly, draped in luminous moss and foxfire. The NARRATOR’s voice resonates like wind through old pines.)**

**NARRATOR:** Three centuries past, beneath a blood-orange moon, the ink dried upon the *Kitsune-Tengu Concord*. Fox-kin emissaries, their silken robes shimmering like captured starlight, laid brush to parchment beside the stern, granite-featured envoys of the Mountain Clans – Tengu hawkmasters and Oni warlords whose shadows swallowed the torchlight. It was a peace woven not from trust, but necessity, a fragile truce halting generations of territorial skirmishes that stained the whispering bamboo forests crimson. The terms were etched in formal courtly script and harsh, angular runes: defined hunting grounds, shared sacred springs, and the solemn vow that claws would not rend fur, nor cunning illusions shatter mountain stone... for as long as the pact held.

For the Kitsune, the Concord became a testament to elegant diplomacy, a web of intricate obligations and subtle favors spun across decades. They speak of it in tea ceremonies, recalling how their ancestors outmaneuvered brute force with silver tongues, securing safety for their vulnerable dens nestled in the lowland valleys. Yet, among the Tengu roosts perched on wind-lashed peaks, the treaty tastes of grudging concession. Elders recount it as chains forged in pragmatism, a restraint upon their rightful dominance, a leash accepted only to staunch the endless drain of warriors lost to the foxes' bewildering tricks and the treacherous lowland fogs. The Oni, ever simmering, remember it as a *burden*, a stifling of their volcanic fury beneath cold parchment.

Now, the original scroll resides deep within the Jade Archive, guarded by spirits sworn to silence. Its once-vibrant ink has faded to ghostly grey, the wax seals – one bearing the delicate paw-print of the Fox Matriarch, the other the brutal, claw-marked sigil of the Mountain Lord – are cracked like ancient riverbeds. The peace endures, yes... but it breathes shallowly, a thin veneer stretched taut over deep, echoing chasms of old resentment and watchful, calculating eyes. Every rustle in the forest canopy, every tremor beneath the mountain, is measured against its brittle terms.
