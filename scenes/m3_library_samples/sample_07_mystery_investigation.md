# Scene 07 — Mystery Investigation

The chill of the treasury seeped through Filomena’s thin gloves as she knelt, the scent of old stone and metal thick in the air. Moonlight, filtered through a single high, barred window, illuminated dust motes dancing over locked chests and stacked ingots. The Head Guard, Kaito, stood rigidly nearby, brow furrowed beneath his horned oni mask. "The seal was intact, Investigator," he rumbled, voice tight with unease. "No one entered. No one *could* have entered without the keys, which never left my person." Yet, beneath the faint scuff-marks of the guards' routine patrols near the door, Filomena’s sharp eyes caught it: a second trail.

These prints were different. Lighter, almost delicate, yet pressed deep into the fine stone dust coating the floor near the jade artifact shelves. They weren't booted feet, but something narrower, elongated – almost clawed at the very tip. And they were faintly luminous, a pale, ash-grey against the darker stone, trailing from the shadowed corner behind a large bronze urn towards the shelves and then... vanishing halfway across the room, as if the walker had simply ceased to exist. Filomena traced a gloved finger near one impossibly deep impression. "These weren't made by mortal feet, Kaito-san," she murmured, her voice low. "And they certainly weren't made by your guards. Someone, or something, walked these stones unseen... and unlocked nothing."

The implications hung heavy. An intruder bypassing physical locks and vigilant oni guards spoke of potent illusion, phasing, or command over shadows. Filomena’s gaze swept the undisturbed artifacts – nothing seemed missing. *Yet.* Why break into a sealed treasury just to walk? The silent, ghostly footprints pointed towards a deeper, more unsettling mystery than mere theft. Who walked where no one could?
