# Scene 05 — Action Aftermath

The battle square is silent now. Aurora kneels beside the shattered ward-stone, the price of victory plain on every face. The air still hums with the residue of broken magic—crackling arcs of foxfire flicker weakly along the cobblestones, dying embers of a spellstorm that had torn through the ranks of oni and hunters alike. The once-pulsing ward-stone lies in obsidian shards, its inner light guttering like a candle in a tomb. She presses a trembling hand to its fractured core, feeling the last whisper of its ancient song fade into stillness. Around her, the survivors move like ghosts, tending to the wounded, lifting the fallen. Blood darkens the stones beneath a crescent moon, and the scent of burnt plum blossoms lingers—Kitsune magic’s final breath.

From the rubble, Rin limps forward, his nine tails ragged, one torn nearly in two. He doesn’t speak, only kneels at her side, placing a clawed hand over hers. The silence stretches, heavy with loss. They had saved the enclave, yes—but the wards were broken, the elders gone, and the veil between worlds now frayed at the edges. Somewhere beyond the torii gate, something new stirred in the dark.

Aurora rises slowly, her silver hair matted with soot and blood. She looks across the square—not at the ruin, but at those still standing. “The stone is dead,” she says, voice low but clear. “But we are not.” And in that breath, the first spark of defiance reignites.
