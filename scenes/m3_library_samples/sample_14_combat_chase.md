# Scene 14 — Combat Chase

Wind whipped through Koibo’s silver-streaked hair as he leapt the narrow gap between tiled rooftops, the stolen scroll clutched tight beneath his arm like a fledgling spirit. Below, fish-sellers shrieked and awnings shuddered as the merchant—plump, red-faced, and roaring in dialect thick with fury—slammed into a clothesline, sending dyed silks fluttering to the cobbles like fallen kites. Koibo grinned, fangs glinting. The guards were faster: one, a hulking oni-blooded brute, shattered a wooden chimney with a misaimed strike; another, nimble and sharp-eyed, scaled a drainpipe with practiced ease.

Tiles cracked underfoot as Koibo pivoted mid-stride, flipping onto a lower awning that groaned but held. The third guard hurled a *kodachi*, the short blade embedding in wood just shy of his heel. The air smelled of salt, incense, and the iron tang of pursuit. With a snarl, Koibo wrenched the blade free and flung it back—not to kill, but to delay. It struck the merchant’s ornate lantern, shattering glass and scattering blue fox-fire across the alley. Shadows writhed. For a heartbeat, the world darkened.

Then—*crack!*—a paper talisman slapped the roof behind him, bursting into crimson flame. The final guard, a quiet *miko* with eyes like frozen jade, chanted under her breath. The fire twisted into serpentine shapes, slithering across the tiles. Koibo didn’t wait. He vaulted the edge, coat flaring like crow’s wings, and dropped into the shadowed mouth of a canal below. Water swallowed him whole. Above, the merchant screamed oaths to forgotten gods, but the scroll was gone—carried now into the whispering dark where the true yokai dwell.
