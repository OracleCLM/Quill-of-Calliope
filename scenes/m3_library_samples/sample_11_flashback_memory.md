# Scene 11 — Flashback Memory

The scent hit Aurora first—pungent pine smoke, thick and cloying, wrapping around her like a suffocating blanket. It should have been comforting, the smell of hearths and winter nights, but beneath it slithered the greasy stink of burning thatch and something worse, something charred and sweet that made her small stomach heave. Screams, raw and ragged, tore through the roar of the flames devouring their mountain village. Embers danced like malevolent fireflies against the ink-black sky.

Her mother’s hand was a desperate anchor, fingers locked tight around Aurora’s own as they were swept along in the panicked river of bodies fleeing the central square. Heat blasted their faces; shadows of running figures flickered monstrously on the burning walls. Then came the jolt—a stumbling man crashing into them. Aurora cried out, her hand wrenched sideways with brutal force. She looked up, searching for her mother’s familiar, fear-wide eyes... but the crushing grip was gone. Only empty air filled her palm. "Mama?" Her thin voice vanished beneath the inferno’s roar and the chilling shrieks of things with too many teeth emerging from the smoke-shrouded treeline. Alone, she stood frozen amidst the fleeing crowd, the terrible scent of burning pine and loss filling her lungs forever.
